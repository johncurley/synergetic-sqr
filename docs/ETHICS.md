# SPU-13 Ethical Guidelines (v2.4.2)
## Responsible Management of High-Dimensional Symmetries

The SPU-13 architecture (The Phi-Core) enables computational operations across 13 symmetric axes. While mathematically elegant, this technology carries significant perceptual and technical risks.

### 1. Perceptual Risk: Topological Vertigo
High-dimensional projections (11D+) exhibit a level of geometric density that can overwhelm human spatial processing. All SPU-11/13 implementations must utilize **Tier-3 Reality Dampening** (heavy super-sampling and luminance clamping) by default.

### 2. Technical Risk: Switching Activity
The 13-axis prime permutation triggers massive gate-switching across the silicon fabric. Improper management can lead to thermal runaway or voltage droop. For this reason, the SPU-13 unit is air-gapped in the RTL from the primary spatial core.

### 3. Ethical Stance: The Human Anchor
Our goal is to assist human discovery, not to replace human spatial intuition with a machine-unfolding abyss. The SPU-13 is a **Restricted Tool** intended for off-line scientific analysis, biological folding, and vacuum simulation, rather than real-time interactive media.
